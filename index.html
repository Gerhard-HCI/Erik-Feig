Critical · Omakase Etiquette

No Fragrance

At Kanda especially, and at every counter dinner this week: please come without cologne, perfume, scented oils, hair products, or strongly fragranced personal care — laundry detergents included.

The chef's composition relies on guests being able to read aroma. A small oversight here can compromise the entire room. This is non-negotiable at counter omakase.

Dining Etiquette

Counter conduct

  • Punctuality is precise

    Omakase meals begin simultaneously for all guests. A meet-and-go time will be confirmed at the hotel before each dinner. At Kanda, the room is released at 20:00 sharp — a single late arrival shifts the chef's rhythm for everyone.

  • No fragrance, no scented products

    No perfume, cologne, scented hair products, scented detergent, gum, or smoking beforehand. This is non-negotiable at omakase counters.

  • Smart-casual is right

    Japan values simplicity. A collared shirt is respectful at omakase. No jacket required. Comfortable shoes — you will walk.

  • Protect the counter

    Sushi counters are often single pieces of Hinoki cypress. Remove rings, bracelets, and watches before sitting.

  • Tipping is not customary

    Don't tip. A sincere "Gochisousama deshita" — "thank you for the meal" — is the right closing.

Connectivity

Staying online in Tokyo

eSIM: Activate before departure. Airalo, HolaFly, and YO all work well in Japan. 4G/5G coverage is excellent across Tokyo.

Suica: Add to Apple Wallet for subway, bus, and most trains. Tap to pay at the gate.

WhatsApp group: Opens forty-eight hours before arrival. All confirmations, time changes, and headcounts flow through this channel. Enable notifications for the trip.

Cash

Yen, ATMs, settlement

Several venues prefer cash for ancillaries — sake supplements, gratuities, small purchases. We recommend each guest carry roughly ¥50,000 in yen for the week.

ATMs at 7-Eleven and Japan Post are the most reliable for international cards. Available at Haneda and Narita on arrival, and any major hotel.

Useful phrases

When language matters

  • Sumimasen — すみません

    "Excuse me" / "I'm sorry." Universal opener.

  • Tasukete kudasai — 助けてください

    "Please help me." For genuine emergencies.

  • Eigo o hanasemasu ka? — 英語を話せますか？

    "Do you speak English?"
